🎥 Tips for the best results click to expand

The analyzer is more accurate the better your clip is framed. None of these are required, but each one meaningfully improves the score.

📏Camera at hip height

Mount the phone at roughly the player’s hip level, not down at ice level or up in the stands. A hip-height camera makes joint angles read true; an extreme up- or down-angle warps every measurement.

🔄True side-on

Stand perpendicular to where the puck will go — not behind, not in front, not at a diagonal. Even 20° off-axis quietly inflates rotation scores and squashes follow-through height.

📐Full body in frame

Skates to head, with ~10% padding on every side, for the whole shot (load → follow-through). If the stick or wrists leave the frame mid-shot, follow-through can’t be scored.

🎥1080p · 60 fps

If your phone can do 1080p at 60 fps, use it. The release-timing metric depends on frame rate — 30 fps caps timing resolution at ~33 ms.

💡Even light, no silhouette

Avoid heavy back-light (window or rink lamps directly behind the player). Pose detection drops landmarks when limbs blur into a dark silhouette.

✂️One shot per clip

Trim to the single attempt — load through follow-through is enough. Extra footage slows the phase detector and makes “load” / “release” picks less reliable.

🧍Just one player

If a teammate skates through the frame the pose tracker may latch onto the wrong person mid-shot. Pause the recording until you have a clear lane.

A coloured quality banner at the top of the results page will call out anything we couldn’t measure on this particular clip.
